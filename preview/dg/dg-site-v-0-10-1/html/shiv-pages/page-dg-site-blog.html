Service Management, ITIL 4 and English Language Skills – A Growing Market in MENA and SE Asia

Author: Sunil Mohal, Ducis Group

Sunil Mohal, founder of Ducis Group – based in Delhi, India – has been involved in IT service management and customer support since the late 1980s. He is an ITIL Expert, Managing Professional and Strategic Leader and accredited trainer for all levels of ITIL 4.

Here, he describes the expanding market in the Middle East, Africa and South-East Asia for training and certification in both ITIL 4 best practice and LanguageCert English exams.

The Growing Demand for ITIL 4 and English Language Skills

The drive toward organizational maturity, operational excellence and a competitive job market is increasing the demand for ITIL 4 and English language certifications in the MENA region and South-East Asia.

Since Ducis Group’s inception in 2017, we have grown to include 14 accredited trainers across India, Kenya, Uganda, UAE, Sri Lanka, Malaysia and Indonesia. Our collaboration with PeopleCert and Axelos has allowed us to tailor our course content to local cultures and languages, leading to substantial growth. In 2021, we administered thousands of exams and 2022 began with a remarkable 50% quarter-on-quarter growth.

The Role of ITIL 4 in Digital Transformation

The widespread use of technology in everyday tasks—like online payments and digital services—requires effective management and improvement of digital technologies. ITIL 4 has become a crucial framework for managing and enhancing IT services, recognized as a standard by companies and professionals across our regions.

The Importance of English Language Proficiency

My career has benefited from being able to communicate effectively in a global language – English. At Ducisgroup, we believe that if you are an ambitious professional working in technology, you need the ability to work with people globally – and that means being able to communicate in a common language.

In business and technology, if you can’t understand what the other person is saying to you, there is a problem! And this counts also for the millions of people in India working in international customer service roles.

Navigating a Competitive Job Market

The younger generation faces a tough job market, requiring a mix of technical, business and language skills. Success in today’s workplace demands both hard and soft skills. Technical qualifications alone are not enough; a combination of best practice certifications, such as ITIL 4 for service management or PRINCE2 for project management and proficiency in English make all the difference.

Conclusion

As the demand for ITIL 4 and English language proficiency continues to grow in the MENA and South-East Asia regions, professionals must prioritize their skills development. The ability to effectively manage IT services and communicate across cultures is essential for success in today’s competitive job market. By investing in training and certification, individuals can enhance their career prospects and contribute to the organizational maturity and operational excellence that businesses seek. Embracing these skills not only opens doors for personal growth but also plays a vital role in driving the digital transformation efforts within their organizations.
